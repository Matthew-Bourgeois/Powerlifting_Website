"About Powerlifting"

Powerlifting is a competitive sport that focuses on the squat, bench press, and deadlift to assess an athlete's overall strength and skill in each lift. The results are determined by a "total," which is the combined weight of the heaviest squat, bench press, and deadlift each athlete completes.

"The 3 Main Lifts"

Squat Tips
  • Stance: Feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly out.
  • Engage Core: Tighten core to protect your back.
  • Lower: Push hips back, bend knees, and squat down.
  • Pause: Briefly stop at the bottom.
  • Return: Push through heels, stand tall.
  • Breathing: Inhale down, exhale up.
  • Tip: Keep weight balanced and knees behind toes.
Deadlift Tips
  • Stance: Feet shoulder-width apart, grip bar outside legs.
  • Engage Core: Tighten core to maintain back position.
  • Lift: Drive through heels, keeping the bar close to your body.
  • Stand Tall: Fully extend hips and knees at the top.
  • Breathing: Inhale before lift, exhale at the top.
  • Tip: Maintain neutral spine throughout the lift.
Bench Press Tips
  • Stance: Feet flat on the ground, back arched slightly.
  • Engage Core: Tighten core to maintain stability.
  • Lower: Control the bar down to the chest.
  • Press: Push the bar up while keeping elbows slightly angled.
  • Breathing: Inhale as you lower, exhale as you press up.
  • Tip: Keep wrists straight and elbows in.
